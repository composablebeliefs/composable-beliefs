# Quote sources verbatim alongside citations

> **Planted** as `agent-behavior:a411`. Under the seed-absorption model
> ([seed-absorption](seed-absorption.md)) this seed's body folds into a `seed` prop on
> a411 and the doc evacuates the nursery; until that mechanism exists it stays here as
> the belief's record.

## The focus (resolved)
When citing a source - a belief id, a numbered section, a file:line - should the agent
quote the load-bearing passage verbatim, or just point at it?

## Resolution
Quote it. Minted `agent-behavior:a411`:
> When citing a source in prose or a thread doc ... quote the load-bearing passage
> verbatim alongside the pointer. Keep the excerpt to the sentence or two the claim rests
> on.

It extends `cb:a475`'s verbatim-leaning doctrine from primitive-authoring to citation:
the pointer enables navigation, the quote enables verification without it.
